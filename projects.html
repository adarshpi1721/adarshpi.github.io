Morphing Foils — Biomimicry of Fish Locomotion

Ongoing research combining chordwise trailing-edge morphing with pitching–heaving motion to replicate fish-like locomotion and improve propulsion. Using ALE-based 2D models and DMD wake analysis, I studied how morph timing affects thrust, efficiency, and wake structure. This work led to thrust increases of ~31% and efficiency gains of ~12%, and was presented at FMFP ’24 and submitted to Physics of Fluids.

Ornithopter

I led the design of a dual-crank active flapping mechanism to replicate bird-like motion. After achieving controlled kinematics, I integrated simple trailing-edge morphing to improve thrust and stability. We achieved a stable 7-second test flight, and this project is what pushed me deeper into biomimicry and flapping-wing aerodynamics.

Suppression of Vortex-Induced Vibrations (VIV)

I worked on passive VIV suppression strategies while helping build and validate a low-speed wind tunnel facility (up to ~96% accuracy). I developed an OpenFOAM solver for 2D VIV, explored passive devices like splitter plates and control cylinders, and compared simulation trends with experimental data.

Ytterbium Beam Collimation — Rarefied Gas Dynamics

My thesis project at IIT Bombay explores how Knudsen-number effects influence beam directionality and flux. Using rarefied-gas models and particle-style simulations, I study how nozzle geometry and background pressure determine beam collimation for applications in biomedical imaging.
