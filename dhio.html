Drone Rotor Flow & Graphene Conduction (Internship)

Summary — At DHIO I applied CFD skills to industry-scale problems using ParticleWorks and ASAP. The work focused on flow near drone rotor tips and conduction in graphene nanoribbons, with an emphasis on producing simulation results that engineers could directly use.

Approach

I worked on two main threads: simulating rotor-tip flows to understand losses and acoustic behavior, and studying heat conduction in graphene-based geometries at relevant operating conditions. My job was to set up robust boundary conditions, run parametric cases, and summarize the trends clearly.

Key observations

The rotor simulations suggested small geometry changes that reduce peak tip velocities and potentially lower noise. The conduction studies showed how ribbon width and edge characteristics influence effective thermal transport, which is important for thermal management design.

What I learned

The internship was a good reality check on how simulations are used in practice: reliability, turnaround time, and clear communication are as important as accuracy. I also got comfortable adapting from academic solvers to industry tools and workflows.
