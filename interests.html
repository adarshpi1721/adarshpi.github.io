Interests

Philosophy

I’ve always been drawn to questions about how we should live, what makes a meaningful life, and why people believe the things they do. I tend to read ethics, existentialism, and philosophy of religion, and I enjoy seeing how different thinkers built complete worldviews from first principles. Some of my favorites are Epictetus, Aristotle, Dostoevsky (who I count as a philosopher in spirit), Emerson, and Schopenhauer. For me, philosophy matters because it directly shapes how we think, act, and grow as people.

Tennis

I’ve been playing tennis since I was a kid, and it’s still one of my favorite ways to unwind. My childhood dream was to win a Grand Slam someday, and although that part didn’t work out, the sport stayed with me. Federer will always be the most enjoyable player to watch, but these days I follow Jannik Sinner pretty closely too.

Reading

Books have shaped my thinking more than anything else. I read a lot of classic literature, but I’ve realized that almost any book becomes meaningful if you try to understand the writer’s mindset—their fears, beliefs, and what they were trying to express. Reading feels like a direct window into another person’s internal world, and I’ve always valued that.

Sketching

I got into drawing during high school and started with semi-realism before moving into anatomy studies. Sketching forces me to slow down and really observe details, which oddly also helps in engineering work where pattern recognition and careful observation matter just as much.

Piano

Piano was a big part of my childhood, and even now I love sitting down to play pieces I enjoy. I still remember learning Für Elise just by following the lights on an old electric keyboard. These days I don’t focus much on improving technique, but I enjoy learning songs I like and playing whenever I get the chance.
